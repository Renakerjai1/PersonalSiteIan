My Tattoos

The tattoo on the left is of a forest nymph, it was the first tattoo that I had ever gotten. Since I have excluded myself from a faith, I had felt that I was spiritually vulnerable. To compensate for my lack of a god, I got her as a protection symbol to keep me safe at all times. The tattoo of Sting on the left is the second, and most recent, tattoo I had gotten. I had seen this available as a stencil and immediately wanted it as a representation of my love of fantasy works. I feel as if tattoos are a way that I can really change my body into something more valuable, not just something to make me stand out. I really fall in line with the traditional Japanese belief of how tattoos should be seen, which is rarely. My body is indeed a canvas that I want to fill, but making my ink easily visible at all times takes away the beauty of its art. I enjoy seeing my tattoos everyday, but I will only show them if someone around me really wants to see them. To show them off would be a shallow form of standing out and I have never really been one to want to stand out for shallow reasons.

Hiren Patel, a talented artist

This is the artist that I last went to and am planning on going to in the future. Hiren specializes in fine detail style tattoos, in which a combination of "ghost lines" are entwined to make up a significant portion of the tattoo. He also works with neo-traditional designs for those who want something that stands out significantly. In general Hiren is a very agreeable and dedicated artist that I would reccomend to anyone who is searching for their first tattoo. Hiren is so light handed that I almost fell asleep while getting Sting done on my arm. Below is a button that will link you to his page, I reccomend him for anyone who is afraid of the pain that comes with a tattoo. I also reccomend him in general, his personality is as great as his talent.

Traditional artists

This is a list of traditional artists that have shaped the way that I like to percieve the world around me. I try to view the landscapes around me as if they were painted by one of these artists to inspire creativity in my daily life.

Nothing helps spark creativity as much as seeing the world through the eyes of such daydreaming artists like Monet and Van Gogh. I often use drawing as a way to express logic while writing programs, I try to accurately portray how a program flow would appear. My old Latin teacher taught me that sometimes when I'm stuck, drawing nonsense can help me see things I could not earlier. Whenever I feel stuck on a program or idea I still use art as an outlet to help me think about all the ways I can do something.
